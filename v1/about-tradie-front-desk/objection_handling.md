# 1. Purpose Of This Document

This document is the complete objection-handling guide for **The Tradie Front Desk System**.

It is designed to help with:

- Sales calls
- Website FAQs
- Proposal documents
- Outreach follow-ups
- Email replies
- Discovery calls
- Audit calls
- Webinar Q&A
- Pricing conversations
- Comparison pages
- Sales training
- Offer refinement

The goal is not to pressure prospects.

The goal is to clearly explain the offer, remove confusion, reduce fear, and help the right trade business owner see why the system is valuable.

A good objection response should do four things:

1. Respect the concern
2. Identify the real fear underneath it
3. Reframe the problem clearly
4. Explain how the system reduces risk or solves the issue

---

# 2. Core Sales Principle

Most objections are not really about the words the prospect says.

They are usually about one of these deeper concerns:

- Fear of wasting money
- Fear of more work
- Fear of technology
- Fear of losing control
- Fear of bad customer experience
- Fear of being locked into something
- Fear of being sold hype
- Fear of being disappointed again
- Lack of trust
- Lack of clarity
- Lack of urgency
- Lack of perceived value

Therefore, do not argue with the surface objection.

Find the real concern underneath it.

Example:

Prospect says:

> “I do not want AI talking to my customers.”

Real concern may be:

> “I do not want my business to sound robotic, unprofessional, or lose control of customer communication.”

Better response:

> “That makes sense. The goal is not to force AI into your business. The core system can start with simple text follow-up, missed-call replies, and owner-controlled handover. AI phone support is optional and only used if it suits your business.”

---

# 3. How To Handle Objections Properly

Use this framework:

## Step 1: Acknowledge

Show the concern is valid.

Example:

> “That makes sense.”

> “I would be cautious about that too.”

> “That is a fair concern.”

---

## Step 2: Clarify

Find the real concern.

Example:

> “When you say you do not want AI, is the main concern that it might sound robotic or say the wrong thing?”

> “Is your concern more about cost, time, or whether it will actually be used?”

---

## Step 3: Reframe

Explain the offer in a better way.

Example:

> “This is not really about AI. It is about making sure enquiries are captured and followed up when you are busy.”

---

## Step 4: Prove Or Explain

Use specifics.

Example:

> “For example, if you miss a call, the system sends a simple text. If someone fills in a quote form, it replies instantly. If they do not respond, it follows up. You stay in control of which jobs you accept.”

---

## Step 5: Confirm

Check if the concern is resolved.

Example:

> “Does that address the part you were worried about?”

# 5. Ads And Marketing Objections

---

## Objection 1: “I do not want to run ads.”

### What They Really Mean

They may be thinking:

- Ads are complicated
- Ads waste money
- I have tried ads before
- I do not want to manage Meta or Google
- I do not want another job
- I do not trust agencies

### What Not To Say

Do not say:

> “It is easy, you can just run them yourself.”

This makes it sound like work.

Do not say:

> “You need ads if you want to grow.”

This creates pressure.

### Best Response

> That makes sense. Most tradies do not want to run ads, and that is not what we are asking you to do. The core system is about capturing enquiries, replying fast, following up, and keeping job opportunities organised. If you want more enquiries later, we can add local campaigns as a managed growth layer, but you do not need to run ads yourself.

### Short Response

> You do not run the ads. Ads are optional. The main system is about capturing and following up with enquiries properly.

### Longer Response

> The way we see it, ads are only useful if the business has a proper system behind them. If calls are missed, forms are not followed up, and quotes are forgotten, then more ads can just create more chaos. So the first step is the front-desk system. Once that is working, if you want more enquiry volume, we can manage simple local campaigns into the system for you.

### Prevention Strategy

Do not lead with ads in website or sales copy.

Use this wording:

> If you need more enquiries, we can also feed the system with local campaigns — but the core offer is your front-desk system, not you running ads.

## Objection 2: “I have tried ads before and they did not work.”

### What They Really Mean

They may have had:

- Bad agency experience
- Poor tracking
- Weak follow-up
- Bad offer
- Slow response
- Low-quality campaign setup

- No system behind the ads

### What Not To Say

> “The last person did it wrong.”

This sounds arrogant.

Do not say:

> “Our ads are better.”

This makes you sound like a normal ad agency.

### Best Response

> That is common. A lot of the time the problem is not only the ad itself. It is what happens after the enquiry comes in. If the customer waits too long, does not get followed up, or the lead sits in an inbox, the ad spend gets wasted. That is why we start with the front-desk system first.

### Short Response

> That is exactly why we do not start by selling ads. We first fix the enquiry capture and follow-up process.

### Longer Response

> Ads can fail for many reasons, but one of the biggest is poor handling after the enquiry. A campaign might create an enquiry, but if the customer is not replied to quickly, if the call is missed, or if the quote is not followed up, it looks like the ads did not work. Our approach is different. We build the system that handles enquiries properly first. Then campaigns become much safer to run because every enquiry has a process.

### Prevention Strategy

Use the line:

> Before spending more money on ads, make sure your business can capture and convert the enquiries it already receives.

## Objection 6: “Will it sound robotic?”

### What They Really Mean

They care about customer trust and brand reputation.

### What Not To Say

Do not say:

> “No, it sounds human.”

This may sound like overpromising.

### Best Response

> The messages are written to be short, plain, and professional. We avoid robotic language. We also get your approval on the tone, so it sounds like a practical trade business response, not a tech company.

### Short Response

> We keep the messages simple, natural, and approved by you before launch.

### Longer Response

> Most customers do not need a long, fancy message. They need to know the business received their enquiry and what the next step is. So the system says things like, “Thanks for your enquiry, what suburb is the job in?” rather than anything complicated or robotic.

### Prevention Strategy

Show message examples during the sales process.

Example:

> Hi Sarah, thanks for reaching out. What suburb is the job in, and what do you need help with?

## Objection 7: “What if the system says the wrong thing?”

### What They Really Mean

They fear losing control or damaging customer relationships.

### What Not To Say

Do not say:

> “It will not.”

No system is perfect.

### Best Response

> That is why we set rules. The system should not give exact pricing, make promises, or accept jobs unless you have approved that. It can capture details, ask basic questions, notify you, and follow up. For anything complex or urgent, it can hand over to you.

### Short Response

> We control what the system can and cannot say. It captures details and escalates when needed.

### Longer Response

> We do not design it to make risky decisions. It is there to help with the first response and follow-up. It can ask where the job is, what service is needed, how urgent it is, and whether photos are available. Pricing, complex advice, and final acceptance stay with you.

### Prevention Strategy

Include guardrails in the offer:

- No exact pricing unless approved
- No promises outside business rules
- Urgent issues escalated
- Owner-approved scripts
- Manual review options
