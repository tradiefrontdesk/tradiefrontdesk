# 1. Purpose Of This Document

This document is the master email, SMS, and follow-up template library for **The Tradie Front Desk System**.

1. **Prospect-facing messages** — messages used to attract, nurture, book, sell, and follow up with trade business owners.

# 2. Messaging Rules

## 2.1 Keep Messages Short And Practical

Trade business owners and their customers do not want long, complicated messages.

Use short paragraphs.

Ask one clear question at a time where possible.

---

## 2.2 Avoid Technical Language

Avoid leading with:

- AI
- CRM
- Automations
- Workflows
- SaaS
- Pipelines, unless explaining simply
- Funnels
- APIs
- Integrations

Use:

- Calls
- Enquiries
- Quote requests
- Follow-up
- Bookings
- Jobs
- Customers
- Front desk
- Missed calls

---

## 2.3 Do Not Overpromise

Avoid:

- Guaranteed jobs
- Guaranteed revenue
- Guaranteed lead numbers
- “Never miss a job again” as a literal claim
- “Customers will always book”

Use:

- Helps capture
- Helps respond
- Helps follow up
- Helps organise
- Helps reduce missed opportunities

---

## 2.4 Respect Opt-Outs

For SMS, email, WhatsApp, and reactivation campaigns, always ensure compliance with relevant consent and unsubscribe/opt-out requirements.

Generic opt-out wording:

> Reply STOP to opt out.

Use where appropriate.

---

## 2.5 Customer-Facing Messages Must Sound Human

Customer-facing system messages should sound like a normal trade business.

Good:

> Hi Sarah, thanks for reaching out. What suburb is the job in, and what do you need help with?

Bad:

> Our automated AI-powered workflow has detected your service request and will now begin the qualification process.

# 33. Message Safety Rules

## 33.1 Do Not Give Exact Pricing Unless Approved

Use:

> The team will review the job details and confirm pricing after they understand the work properly.

## 33.2 Do Not Confirm Jobs Automatically Unless Approved

Use:

> We’ll review the details and confirm the next step.

## 33.3 Do Not Over-Follow-Up

Keep follow-up polite and limited.

## 33.4 Stop Follow-Up When Customer Replies

Any customer reply should stop no-response sequences.

## 33.5 Avoid Aggressive Sales Language

Avoid:

- Last chance
- You must act now
- We are waiting on you
- This offer expires today

Use:

- Just checking in
- Let us know if you have questions
- Happy to help if still needed
