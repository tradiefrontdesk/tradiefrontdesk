# Features & Benefits Document

# The Tradie Front Desk System

## Turning System Features Into Buyer-Facing Value

## Core Promise

# **More Booked Jobs. Less Chasing. No Tech Headaches.**

---

# 1. Purpose Of This Document

This document translates the features of **The Tradie Front Desk System** into clear benefits that matter to trade business owners.

A feature is what the system does.

A benefit is why the customer cares.

Trade business owners do not buy features like CRM pipelines, automations, lead forms, AI voice agents, or workflows.

They buy outcomes like:

- More booked jobs
- Faster replies
- Fewer missed opportunities
- Less chasing
- Better organisation
- Less admin
- More control
- More confidence
- Better customer experience

This document should be used for:

- Website copy
- Sales pages
- Proposals
- Sales calls
- Ads
- Email campaigns
- Webinar scripts
- Offer documents
- Comparison pages
- Objection handling
- Explainer videos
- Landing pages

The goal is to ensure every feature is presented in plain, practical, tradie-friendly language.

---

# 2. Core Positioning Rule

Do not sell the tool.

Sell the job it does for the business.

Instead of saying:

> We set up automations in your CRM.

Say:

> When a customer enquires, the system replies, follows up, and keeps the opportunity moving without you chasing it manually.

Instead of saying:

> We install an AI voice agent.

Say:

> When you cannot answer the phone, the system can still capture the customer’s details and make sure the enquiry does not go cold.

Instead of saying:

> We create a pipeline.

Say:

> You can see which enquiries need a call, which quotes need follow-up, and which jobs are ready to book.

---

# 3. Feature-To-Benefit Framework

For every feature, use this structure:

1. **Feature** — What it is
2. **Plain-English Explanation** — How to explain it to a tradie
3. **Practical Benefit** — What it helps them do
4. **Emotional Benefit** — How it makes them feel
5. **Financial Benefit** — How it may protect or create revenue
6. **Customer Experience Benefit** — How it improves the customer’s experience
7. **Sales Angle** — How to sell it
8. **Example Copy** — Ready-to-use language
9. **Objections It Helps Solve** — Which objections this feature reduces
10. **Best Use Case** — When it matters most

# 5. Feature 1: Front Desk Audit

## Feature

A structured review of the trade business’s current enquiry process.

## Plain-English Explanation

We check what happens when someone calls, fills in a form, asks for a quote, messages the business, or goes quiet after a quote.

We look for places where jobs may be slipping through the cracks.

## Practical Benefit

The business sees where enquiries are being missed, delayed, forgotten, or poorly followed up.

It gives the owner a clear starting point instead of guessing what to fix.

## Emotional Benefit

The owner feels seen and understood.

They realise the problem is not that they are bad at business. The problem is that they do not have a clear enquiry system.

## Financial Benefit

Finding one major leak, such as missed calls or unfollowed quotes, can reveal potential lost revenue.

## Customer Experience Benefit

Fixing the gaps means customers get faster replies and clearer next steps.

## Sales Angle

> Before we recommend more leads, ads, or automation, we first find where your current enquiries are leaking.

## Example Copy

> Book a Free Front Desk Audit and we will show you where your trade business may be losing jobs from missed calls, slow replies, poor follow-up, or messy enquiry handling.

## Objections It Helps Solve

- “I do not know if I need this.”
- “I already get leads.”
- “I have tried marketing before.”
- “What would you actually fix?”

## Best Use Case

Front-end lead magnet, sales calls, website CTA, outreach offer.
