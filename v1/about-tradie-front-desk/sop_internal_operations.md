# 2. Operating Philosophy

## 2.1 The Product Must Be Repeatable

The agency should not build a completely new system for every client.

The product must be standardised around:

- Core pipeline
- Core forms
- Core workflows
- Core message templates
- Core reporting
- Core delivery stages
- Core QA checklist

Customisation should happen in controlled areas:

- Business services
- Service areas
- Good-fit jobs
- Bad-fit jobs
- Urgency rules
- Message tone
- Notification recipients
- Follow-up timing
- Optional add-ons

---

## 2.2 The Client Bought An Outcome, Not Complexity

Clients do not care about:

- Workflow logic
- Custom field names
- Snapshot structure
- API settings
- Internal tags
- Trigger conditions

Clients care about:

- Are enquiries captured?
- Do customers get replies?
- Are missed calls followed up?
- Are quotes followed up?
- Does the owner know what to do?
- Is the system working?

Internal work should be technically solid, but client communication should stay outcome-focused.

---

## 2.3 Done-For-You Means Low Client Burden, Not Zero Client Input

The system is done-for-you, but the client still must provide:

- Business details
- Services
- Service areas
- Good-fit rules
- Bad-fit rules
- Access
- Message approval
- Feedback

The client cannot expect a good build if they do not provide accurate information.

Internal rule:

> If client information is missing, do not guess risky business rules. Ask for clarification or use safe manual review defaults.

---

## 2.4 Protect The Core Promise

Every operational decision should protect the core promise:

# **More Booked Jobs. Less Chasing. No Tech Headaches.**

This means:

- Do not overcomplicate client experience
- Do not over-automate risky decisions
- Do not create too many notifications
- Do not use robotic customer messages
- Do not sell unmanaged complexity
- Do not allow scope creep to break delivery
- Do not launch untested workflows

---

Messages must help customers take the next step without sounding robotic or pushy.

---

## 10.2 Message Standards

Messages should be:

- Short
- Clear
- Friendly
- Practical
- Easy to reply to
- Not overly formal
- Not overly casual
- Not technical
- Not risky

---

## 10.3 Customer Message Rules

Do not:

- Give exact pricing unless approved
- Promise availability unless approved
- Confirm jobs automatically unless approved
- Decline leads unless approved
- Use robotic AI language
- Send excessive follow-ups

# 18. Issue Escalation SOP

## 18.1 Critical Issues

Critical issues include:

- Customer messages going to wrong people
- Duplicate spam messages
- Missed calls not triggering
- Forms broken
- Client not receiving urgent notifications
- AI phone giving wrong/risky information
- Campaign leads not routing

---

## 18.2 Escalation Steps

1. Acknowledge issue
2. Pause affected workflow if needed
3. Investigate logs
4. Identify root cause
5. Fix issue
6. Test fix
7. Notify client
8. Document issue and fix
9. Update SOP if needed

---

## 18.3 Client Message For Issue

> Thanks for letting us know. We’re reviewing this now. If needed, we’ll pause the affected workflow while we check the issue, then confirm once it has been fixed and tested.

# 22. Churn Prevention SOP

## 22.1 Why Clients Churn

Clients may churn because:

- They do not see value
- They do not understand what the system is doing
- They expected guaranteed jobs
- They are not receiving reports
- The system is not updated
- They are not using the pipeline
- They have cashflow issues
- Support is slow
- Scope expectations were unclear

---

## 22.2 Churn Prevention Actions

- Send monthly reports
- Show captured enquiries
- Show missed-call recovery
- Show follow-up activity
- Ask for feedback
- Recommend improvements
- Clarify wins
- Keep communication proactive
- Fix issues quickly
- Avoid overpromising in sales

---

## 22.3 Save Call Script

> I understand you are considering cancelling. Before we make a decision, can I ask what is driving it — cost, results, system usage, support, or something else?

Then diagnose.

If cost:

> We may be able to reduce scope temporarily rather than cancelling everything.

If results:

> Let’s look at the enquiry volume, missed-call activity, follow-up activity, and whether the business has enough opportunities coming in.

If usage:

> We may need to simplify the system or adjust notifications.

---

- Sales
- Onboarding
- Build
- QA
- Client review
- Launch
- Support
- Monthly management
- Change requests

## 24.2 Watch For Scope Creep

Scope creep indicators:

- Client asks for frequent changes
- Client wants custom features not sold
- Client asks for website work in system package
- Client expects marketing strategy beyond package
- Client wants unlimited meetings
- Client asks for new campaigns without paying

---

## 24.3 Package Margin Rules

### Front Desk Starter

Keep simple.

Limit customisation.

Avoid complex integrations.

### Booked Jobs System

Allow moderate customisation.

Protect monthly support time.

### Managed Growth System

Allow higher support and optimisation.

Charge properly for campaign and AI complexity.

## 24.4 Monthly Support Limits

Define reasonable monthly support per package.

### Front Desk Starter

- Basic support
- Small changes only
- No strategy calls unless included
