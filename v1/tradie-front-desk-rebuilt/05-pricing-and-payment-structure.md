# Pricing And Payment Structure

## Pricing Philosophy

Tradie Front Desk should not be priced like:

- cheap CRM software
- a chatbot
- a freelancer automation setup
- pay-per-lead
- a small website widget
- a basic ad campaign

It should be priced like:

**a managed front-desk system that protects and converts job opportunities.**

The value is not the tool. The value is the business function:

- fewer missed calls
- faster replies
- better quote follow-up
- more booked callbacks or site visits
- reactivated old opportunities
- better reviews
- less owner chasing
- more organised pipeline

## Price Anchors

Anchor against:

- one missed job
- one lost quote
- one missed roof replacement
- one urgent plumbing callout
- one switchboard upgrade
- one wasted ad spend month
- part-time admin cost
- the owner's after-hours time

Do not anchor against:

- GoHighLevel subscription cost
- SMS tool cost
- cheap CRM plans
- freelancer hourly rates
- chatbot widgets

## Recommended Public Pricing Strategy

For the website, show enough pricing clarity to avoid feeling evasive.

Recommended public format:

- "Packages start from..." with real starting prices visible
- "Final pricing depends on enquiry sources, number of locations, AI phone usage, campaigns, and integrations."
- "Book a Free Front Desk Audit to confirm the best fit."

Show starting prices and package ranges so buyers can tell whether this is in budget before they book.

Recommended public starting anchors:

- Front Desk Starter: from `$1,500 setup + $497/month`
- Booked Jobs System: from `$2,500 setup + $1,200/month`
- Managed Growth System: from `$4,000 setup + $2,000/month + ad spend`

Founding-client campaigns can use lower entry pricing, but the public pricing page should still make the likely investment level clear.

## Payment Options

Keep payment options simple. Too many choices weaken the offer.

### Option 1: Standard Setup Plus Monthly

Use this as the default.

Structure:

- setup fee paid before build starts
- monthly fee starts at launch or agreed start date
- ad spend paid directly by client where applicable

Best for:

- most clients
- clean delivery
- clear scope

Sales line:

> The setup covers building and launching your system. The monthly covers management, support, monitoring, and optimisation.

### Option 2: Split Setup Payment

Use this when cash flow is the only blocker.

Structure:

- 50 percent setup deposit
- 50 percent before launch, or split over the first two months
- monthly management still applies

Best for:

- good-fit clients with timing concerns

Rule:

Do not reduce price first. Split payment before discounting.

### Option 3: Quarterly Or Annual Prepay

Use this for stronger clients or founding-client campaigns.

Structure:

- setup fee paid upfront
- three, six, or twelve months prepaid
- small discount or bonus may be included

Recommended incentives:

- free review builder setup
- reactivation mini-campaign
- extra website enquiry audit
- one additional message sequence

Avoid deep discounts. Add value before cutting price.

## Payment Terms

Use these as commercial defaults unless a proposal states otherwise:

- accepted methods: bank transfer, card, or direct debit
- setup invoice due: before build work starts
- monthly billing starts: at launch or on the agreed monthly start date, whichever is listed in the proposal
- minimum term: 3 months for Starter, 3 to 6 months for Booked Jobs, 6 months for Managed Growth unless otherwise agreed
- cancellation notice: 30 days after the minimum term
- late payment: service, campaign management, and non-critical support can be paused until payment is current
- ad spend, SMS, phone, AI, and platform usage costs: paid by the client separately unless the proposal states otherwise

Monthly support includes a defined fair-use allowance. Recommended default:

- Starter: up to 2 small message or workflow changes per month
- Booked Jobs: up to 4 small message or workflow changes per month
- Managed Growth: up to 6 small message or workflow changes per month

Anything beyond the included allowance is quoted separately or scheduled as a paid optimisation sprint.

## Done-For-You, Done-With-You, And Build-And-Hand-Off Pricing Logic

### Done-For-You

Highest value and default model.

Charge:

- full setup
- full monthly management

Best for:

- non-technical tradies
- busy owners
- businesses that want the result managed

### Done-With-You

Moderate setup and lower monthly, only when the client has internal admin capacity.

Charge:

- setup and training
- lighter monthly support

Risk:

- client may not execute consistently

Use only if:

- they have a reliable office/admin person
- they want internal ownership
- they understand missed follow-up becomes their responsibility

### Build-And-Hand-Off

Lowest strategic priority.

Charge:

- higher one-time setup
- optional support retainer

Use only for:

- simple builds
- clients with technical/admin capability
- specific one-off workflows

Avoid making this the main public offer because the business should be a managed system provider, not a one-off automation shop.

## Guarantee

Use a process guarantee, not a results guarantee.

### Recommended Guarantee

**Front Desk Launch Guarantee**

> Once we have your business details, services, access, and approvals, we guarantee your agreed front-desk system will be built, tested, and launched according to the proposal. If a proposal-listed part of the core setup is not working, we fix it.

### Stronger Version

**Live System Guarantee**

> If we do not get the agreed core enquiry capture, response, and follow-up system live after receiving all required access and information, we continue setup work at no extra charge until it is live.

### Guarantee Conditions

The guarantee only applies when:

- the client provides required access, business details, service details, and approvals on time
- the workflow or system item is specifically listed in the proposal
- delays are not caused by client approvals, missing assets, disconnected accounts, unpaid invoices, or third-party outages

Client-caused delays pause the guarantee clock.

Excluded from the guarantee:

- third-party platform outages
- ad platform disapprovals
- phone carrier delays
- client account access issues
- changes requested outside the agreed scope
- market outcomes such as exact job numbers or revenue

### Do Not Guarantee

Do not guarantee:

- exact job numbers
- exact revenue
- exact profit
- exact lead volume
- exact ad performance
- exact review count
- exact conversion rate
- that every customer replies
- that every quote is accepted

Website line:

> We guarantee the system we control, not market outcomes no one can honestly guarantee.

## Scope Boundaries

Every proposal should define:

- package selected
- channels connected
- included workflows
- included message sequences
- whether AI phone is included
- whether campaigns are included
- whether website work is included
- whether integrations are included
- monthly support allowance
- usage costs
- client responsibilities

Client responsibilities:

- provide accurate business details
- approve scripts
- provide access
- respond to escalated serious enquiries
- keep service/pricing promises accurate
- pay platform, SMS, phone, AI, and ad costs where applicable

## Scope Exclusions

Unless specifically listed in the proposal, monthly management does not include:

- unlimited content changes
- unlimited message rewrites
- unlimited integrations
- custom development outside the proposal
- ad management outside included campaigns
- SEO campaigns
- full website rebuilds
- job-management software support outside the enquiry stage
- manual receptionist service
- manual outbound calling
- unlimited database cleanup
- complex multi-location routing
