# 1. Purpose Of This Document

This document defines how **The Tradie Front Desk System** works as a product, service, and operational delivery system.

It explains the full journey from a new customer enquiry through to booked quote, follow-up, pipeline tracking, and optional growth campaigns.

This document should be used to guide:

- Internal delivery
- Product development
- Website copy
- Sales explanations
- Onboarding forms
- Automation builds
- Client proposals
- Workflow diagrams
- SOPs
- Training material
- Future AI agent prompts
- CRM/pipeline setup

The purpose of this document is to make the product extremely clear.

The business should never sound like it is selling random automations, AI tools, CRM features, or advertising services. It is selling one clear system:

> A managed front-desk system that captures enquiries, responds instantly, follows up automatically, and helps turn quote requests into booked jobs.

---

# 2. Product Overview

## 2.1 What The Product Is

**The Tradie Front Desk System** is a done-for-you enquiry management, response, follow-up, and booking system for trade businesses.

It acts as a smart front desk for businesses that are too busy to manually answer, chase, organise, and follow up every enquiry.

The system connects the main ways customers contact the business and creates a structured process for handling them.

It helps ensure that enquiries are:

1. Captured
2. Responded to quickly
3. Qualified
4. Routed or booked
5. Followed up
6. Tracked
7. Reported on

---

## 2.2 What The Product Is Not

The product is not:

- A simple chatbot
- A generic AI agent
- A pure advertising service
- A pay-per-lead offer
- A shared lead service
- A DIY CRM platform
- A website-only service
- A receptionist replacement in every situation
- A full business operations system
- A guarantee of exact job numbers
- A replacement for good customer service
- A replacement for the owner’s business judgement

The product supports and improves the business’s enquiry handling process. It does not magically fix every part of the business.

---

## 2.3 The Plain-English Explanation

When someone contacts a trade business, the system makes sure they are not ignored.

If they call and the business misses the call, the system can text them back.

If they fill in a quote form, the system can respond instantly.

If they do not reply, the system can follow up.

If they are interested, the system can collect job details and help book a callback, quote, or site visit.

If a quote has been sent, the system can follow up.

If the business has old leads or past customers, the system can help re-contact them.

The owner does not have to manually chase everything from memory.

# 3. Core Product Philosophy

The system is built around several principles.

---

## 3.1 The System Must Feel Simple

Trade business owners do not want complexity.

The system must be easy to explain, easy to use, and easy to understand.

The owner should not have to learn:

- Ad platforms
- CRM settings
- Automation logic
- AI prompts
- Technical workflows
- Funnel systems
- API integrations

The owner should understand only what matters:

- Where enquiries come in
- What happens next
- How they are notified
- How jobs are tracked
- What needs their attention
- How to request changes

---

## 3.2 The System Must Reduce Work

The product should not feel like another job.

It should reduce:

- Missed calls
- Manual replies
- Manual chasing
- Forgotten quotes
- Scattered messages
- Repetitive admin
- Lead confusion
- After-hours follow-up

If the product creates more work than it removes, the offer becomes weaker.

---

## 3.3 The System Must Keep The Owner In Control

The system should assist the owner, not replace them.

The owner must stay in control of:

- Which jobs to accept
- Which areas to service
- Which job types to avoid
- When to call customers
- Whether to use AI voice
- How aggressive follow-up should be
- How customers are handed over
- What promises are made

The system can qualify, organise, and follow up, but it should not make risky commitments unless approved.

---

## 3.4 The System Must Be Trade-Specific

A generic CRM or automation setup is not enough.

The system must understand trade business realities:

- Calls happen while the owner is on the tools
- Customers often want quotes
- Customers may call multiple businesses
- Photos may be needed
- Location matters
- Urgency matters
- Job type matters
- Some jobs are not worth taking
- Quotes need follow-up
- Reviews matter
- Repeat customers matter
- Old customers can be reactivated

---

## 3.5 The System Must Be Built Around The Enquiry Journey

The system is not built around software features.

It is built around the customer journey:

1. Customer has a problem
2. Customer contacts the trade business
3. The business must respond quickly
4. The customer must feel acknowledged
5. The right job details must be collected
6. The enquiry must move to the next step
7. Follow-up must happen if the customer goes quiet
8. The opportunity must be tracked
9. The job is either won, lost, or unsuitable

---

# 4. The Core Mechanism

The entire system can be explained in five words:

# **Capture → Respond → Qualify → Book → Follow Up**

This mechanism should be repeated across sales material, website copy, onboarding, and delivery documents.

---

## 4.1 Capture

The system captures customer enquiries from key channels.

Possible capture sources:

- Website contact forms
- Quote request forms
- Phone calls
- Missed calls
- Landing pages
- Facebook lead forms
- Google lead forms
- Email enquiries
- SMS messages
- WhatsApp messages
- Facebook messages
- Instagram messages
- Old leads
- Past customers
- Paid campaign enquiries

The goal is to stop enquiries from being scattered or forgotten.

Buyer-facing explanation:

> Every new opportunity has a place to go.

---

## 4.2 Respond

The system responds quickly after an enquiry is captured.

Response methods may include:

- SMS
- Email
- WhatsApp
- AI phone assistant
- Missed-call text-back
- Internal notification to the owner

The goal is to stop the customer feeling ignored.

Buyer-facing explanation:

> Customers get a fast reply, even when you are on the tools.

---

## 4.3 Qualify

The system collects useful information about the job.

Qualification questions may include:

- What service do you need?
- Where are you located?
- Is this urgent?
- When do you need the work done?
- Are you the property owner?
- Can you upload photos?
- What is the approximate size of the job?
- Have you had quotes already?
- What is the best time to call?
- Is this residential, commercial, or rural?

The goal is to help the business understand whether the enquiry is worth pursuing.

Buyer-facing explanation:

> Get the right details upfront so you are not wasting time chasing unclear enquiries.

---

## 4.4 Book

The system helps the customer move to the next step.

Possible next steps:

- Book a quote appointment
- Request a callback
- Book a site visit
- Send photos
- Confirm location
- Speak to the owner
- Get routed to urgent response
- Get added to a follow-up sequence
- Be marked as not suitable

The goal is to move the enquiry forward rather than letting it sit idle.

Buyer-facing explanation:

> Turn more quote requests into actual appointments or next steps.

---

## 4.5 Follow Up

The system follows up with customers who do not respond, have not booked, or have received a quote.

Follow-up types:

- New enquiry follow-up
- Missed call follow-up
- No response follow-up
- Quote follow-up
- Estimate follow-up
- Old lead follow-up
- Past customer follow-up
- Review request follow-up

The goal is to stop good opportunities from going cold.

Buyer-facing explanation:

> The system keeps following up so you do not have to remember every lead manually.

- Speed of response
- Quote request process

- Google review process

## Output

1. What is working

## Sales Role

The audit is the front-end offer and sales tool.

It helps the prospect see the problem before being pitched the solution.

# Module 2: Enquiry Capture System

## Purpose

To capture incoming enquiries from multiple sources and place them into one structured system.

## Inputs

- Website forms
- Quote request forms
- Missed calls
- Phone calls
- Landing pages
- Facebook lead forms
- Google lead forms
- Manual imports
- Existing customer databases
- Campaign leads

## Outputs

- New contact created
- New opportunity created
- Pipeline stage assigned
- Owner notified
- Customer receives response
- Follow-up sequence triggered

## Key Rules

- Every enquiry should create or update a contact
- Every qualified enquiry should enter the pipeline

- The owner should receive a clear notification
- The customer should receive a fast acknowledgement

## Buyer Benefit

> No more scattered enquiries sitting across calls, emails, texts, forms, and memory.

---

# Module 3: Missed Call Saver

## Purpose

To recover opportunities when the business misses a phone call.

## Trigger

A call is missed or unanswered.

## System Action

The system sends an automatic SMS to the caller.

## Example Customer SMS

> Hi {{first_name}}, thanks for calling {{business_name}}. We may be on-site at the moment. Reply with what you need help with and we’ll get back to you as soon as possible.

## Optional Follow-Up

If the caller replies, the system can ask for:

- Job type
- Location
- Urgency
- Photos
- Best callback time

If the caller does not reply, a follow-up can be sent later.

## Owner Notification

The owner receives a notification such as:

> Missed call from {{contact.name}} - {{contact.phone}}. Auto-reply sent. Waiting for customer response.

## Buyer Benefit

> If you miss the call, the opportunity does not immediately go cold.

---

# Module 4: Instant Enquiry Response

## Purpose

To respond instantly to new enquiries from forms, landing pages, ads, or messages.

## Trigger

A customer submits a quote request, website form, or lead form.

## System Action

The customer receives an instant acknowledgement.

## Example Customer SMS

> Hi {{first_name}}, thanks for your enquiry. We’ve received your request and will help you with the next step. To make sure we send this to the right person, can you quickly confirm the job location and what you need done?

## Example Customer Email

Subject:

> We received your enquiry

Body:

> Hi {{first_name}}, thanks for contacting {{business_name}}. We have received your enquiry and will review the details shortly. If you have photos or extra information about the job, you can reply to this email or text us directly.

## Owner Notification

The owner receives details of the enquiry.

## Buyer Benefit

> Customers know they have been heard immediately, even if you cannot reply personally right away.

---

# Module 5: Quote Request Form

## Purpose

To collect the right information from customers upfront.

## Recommended Form Fields

### Basic Details

- Full name
- Phone number
- Email
- Suburb/location
- Preferred contact method

### Job Details

- Type of service needed
- Short description of the job
- Urgency
- Residential or commercial
- Property type
- Photos or file upload
- Preferred time for callback or quote

### Qualification Fields

- Are you the property owner or decision maker?
- Have you had quotes already?
- When would you like the job completed?
- Is this an emergency?
- Approximate budget range, if appropriate

## Form Design Principles

- Keep the form simple enough to complete
- Only ask what is useful
- Use conditional questions where possible
- Allow photo uploads for trades where visual inspection helps
- Make phone number required
- Make location required
- Avoid too many unnecessary fields

## Buyer Benefit

> Better job details upfront means fewer wasted calls and faster qualification.

---

# Module 6: Lead Qualification Flow

## Purpose

To collect enough information to determine whether the enquiry is a good fit.

## Qualification Categories

### Location Fit

Is the customer inside the service area?

### Service Fit

Does the business offer the requested service?

### Urgency Fit

Is it urgent, normal, or future work?

### Value Fit

Is the job likely to be worth the business’s time?

### Decision-Maker Fit

Is the person able to approve the work?

### Timing Fit

Does the customer need work within a realistic timeframe?

## Possible Outcomes

- Good fit: move to callback or booking
- Needs review: notify owner
- Bad fit: polite decline or manual review
- Urgent: notify owner immediately
- Incomplete: request more details

## Buyer Benefit

> Spend less time chasing enquiries that are not suitable.

---

# Module 7: Booking Or Callback System

## Purpose

To help move qualified enquiries toward a real next step.

## Booking Options

Depending on the business, the system may offer:

- Book a callback
- Book a quote appointment
- Book a site visit
- Request a quote review
- Request urgent contact
- Request manual approval before booking

## Recommended Approach For Tradies

For many trade businesses, direct calendar booking can be risky if job type, travel time, and job size vary.

Therefore, the default should often be:

> Request a callback or quote time, then allow the owner/business to confirm.

This keeps control with the business.

## Buyer Benefit

> Customers are guided to the next step without giving the system too much control.

---

# Module 8: Follow-Up Engine

## Purpose

To follow up with customers who have not responded or have not taken the next step.

## Follow-Up Types

### New Enquiry Follow-Up

For customers who submitted a form but have not replied.

### Missed Call Follow-Up

For customers who called but did not respond to the missed-call text.

### Quote Request Follow-Up

For customers who started but did not complete the quote process.

### Quote Sent Follow-Up

For customers who received a quote but have not accepted or declined.

### No-Response Follow-Up

For customers who went quiet.

### Old Lead Follow-Up

For older enquiries or previous quotes.

## Example New Enquiry Follow-Up Sequence

### Immediate

> Hi {{first_name}}, thanks for your enquiry. We’ve received it and will help with the next step.

### 1 Hour Later

> Hi {{first_name}}, just checking we have the right details. What suburb is the job in, and what do you need help with?

### Next Day

> Hi {{first_name}}, just following up on your enquiry with {{business_name}}. Are you still looking for help with this job?

### 3 Days Later

> Hi {{first_name}}, we have not heard back, so we will leave this for now. If you still need help, just reply here and we can pick it back up.

## Buyer Benefit

> Follow-up happens automatically instead of relying on the owner remembering every lead.

---

# Module 9: Quote Follow-Up System

## Purpose

To improve conversion after quotes or estimates have been sent.

## Trigger

A job moves to “Quote Sent” stage.

## Follow-Up Examples

### 24 Hours After Quote

> Hi {{first_name}}, just checking you received the quote from {{business_name}}. Let us know if you have any questions.

### 3 Days After Quote

> Hi {{first_name}}, just following up to see if you would like to go ahead or if there is anything you would like clarified.

### 7 Days After Quote

> Hi {{first_name}}, we will leave this with you for now. If you would like to move forward, reply here and we can help with the next step.

## Important Rule

Quote follow-up messages should be polite, not pushy.

## Buyer Benefit

> Stop sending quotes and hoping. Keep the conversation moving professionally.

## Purpose

To show where every opportunity sits.

2. Auto Response Sent
3. Details Requested
4. Details Received
5. Needs Owner Review
6. Callback Required
7. Quote / Site Visit Booked
8. Quote Sent
9. Follow-Up Needed
10. Won
11. Lost
12. Not Suitable

## Stage Definitions

### New Enquiry

A new lead has entered the system.

### Auto Response Sent

The system has replied to the customer.

### Details Requested

The system has asked for more job details.

### Details Received

The customer has provided enough information.

### Needs Owner Review

The business owner or team needs to review the enquiry.

### Callback Required

The customer needs to be contacted.

### Quote / Site Visit Booked

A quote, callback, or visit has been arranged.

### Quote Sent

A quote has been sent and follow-up should begin.

### Follow-Up Needed

The customer has not replied or a follow-up task is due.

### Won

The job has been accepted or booked.

### Lost

The job did not proceed.

### Not Suitable

The enquiry was not a good fit.

## Buyer Benefit

> Know what is happening with every opportunity instead of keeping it all in your head.

# Module 15: Optional AI Phone Assistant

## Purpose

To help answer calls, qualify enquiries, or capture job details when the business cannot answer.

## Use Cases

- After-hours call answering
- Missed-call callback
- Basic enquiry qualification
- Job detail collection
- Booking request
- FAQ answering
- Routing urgent jobs

## Important Guardrails

The AI phone assistant should:

- Clearly represent the business
- Be polite and concise
- Ask one question at a time
- Avoid promising exact prices unless approved
- Avoid making commitments outside its rules
- Escalate urgent or complex cases
- Capture details accurately
- Not pretend to be the owner
- Not argue with customers
- Not over-explain

## Buyer-Facing Positioning

Do not lead with:

> We give you an AI voice agent.

Better:

> When you cannot answer, the system can still capture customer details and keep the enquiry moving.

## Buyer Benefit

> Customers are not left hanging when you are unavailable.

# 6. Customer Journey Scenarios

This section explains how the system works in real-life situations.

---

## Scenario 1: Missed Phone Call

### Situation

A customer calls while the tradie is on-site and cannot answer.

### Without The System

- The call is missed
- The customer may call another business
- The owner may forget to call back
- The opportunity may disappear

### With The System

1. Customer calls
2. Call is missed
3. System sends instant SMS
4. Customer replies with job details
5. Contact is created or updated
6. Opportunity enters pipeline
7. Owner receives notification
8. System follows up if customer does not reply

### Outcome

The missed call is not wasted.

---

## Scenario 2: Website Quote Request

### Situation

A customer visits the website and submits a quote request.

### Without The System

- Form goes to email
- Owner may not see it quickly
- Customer waits
- Follow-up may be delayed
- Job may go to competitor

### With The System

1. Customer fills in quote form
2. Contact is created
3. Opportunity enters pipeline
4. Customer receives instant SMS/email
5. System asks for missing details if needed
6. Owner receives clear notification
7. Customer is moved toward callback or booking
8. Follow-up continues automatically

### Outcome

The enquiry is acknowledged and moved forward quickly.

---

## Scenario 3: Customer Goes Quiet

### Situation

A customer enquires but does not respond after the first message.

### Without The System

- Owner may forget
- Lead goes cold
- Customer books someone else

### With The System

1. Customer goes quiet
2. Follow-up sequence begins
3. Customer receives polite reminders
4. If customer replies, the opportunity reactivates
5. If customer does not reply, the lead is marked accordingly

### Outcome

The system gives the opportunity a fair chance without manual chasing.

---

## Scenario 4: Quote Sent But Not Accepted

### Situation

The business sends a quote but hears nothing back.

### Without The System

- Owner may feel awkward following up
- Customer may forget
- Quote may be lost
- No clear tracking

### With The System

1. Job is moved to Quote Sent
2. Quote follow-up sequence starts
3. Customer receives polite check-ins
4. Owner is notified if customer replies
5. Pipeline shows status clearly

### Outcome

More quotes are followed up professionally.

---

## Scenario 5: Old Lead Reactivation

### Situation

The business has old enquiries that never booked.

### Without The System

### With The System

1. Old contacts are imported or segmented
2. Reactivation message is sent
3. Interested contacts reply

### Outcome

Old leads can become new opportunities.

---

## Scenario 6: Paid Campaign Enquiry

The business wants more enquiries and runs a local campaign.

### Without The System

### With The System

Ad enquiries are handled properly instead of being wasted.

---

Actions:

---

Actions:

1. Stop no-response follow-up
2. Update contact notes
3. Notify owner if human attention needed
4. Ask next qualifying question if automated flow applies
5. Move pipeline stage accordingly

---

## Trigger 4: Lead Form Submitted From Campaign

Actions:

1. Create/update contact
2. Create opportunity
3. Assign source: Campaign
4. Send instant response
5. Ask qualifying questions
6. Notify owner
7. Start follow-up

---

## Trigger 5: Opportunity Moved To Quote Sent

Actions:

1. Start quote follow-up sequence
2. Create follow-up task if needed
3. Notify owner after set period if no response

---

## Trigger 6: Opportunity Marked Won

Actions:

1. Stop active lead follow-up
2. Tag as customer
3. Optionally schedule review request
4. Optionally add to past customer list

---

## Trigger 7: Opportunity Marked Lost

Actions:

1. Stop follow-up
2. Tag reason if available
3. Optionally add to future reactivation list

# 11. Message And Tone Rules

All customer-facing messages should be:

- Short
- Clear
- Friendly
- Professional
- Helpful
- Not overly salesy
- Not robotic
- Not too casual
- Easy to reply to

Avoid:

- Long paragraphs
- Too many questions at once
- Pushy sales language
- Fake urgency
- Overuse of emojis
- Overly technical wording
- Promising exact prices unless approved

---

> Hello valued customer! Our advanced AI-powered automation system has detected your enquiry and will now proceed through our intelligent qualification workflow to optimise your service journey.

- Feels unnatural
- Does not sound like a trade business

# 16. Guardrails And Limitations

## 16.1 The System Should Not Promise Exact Prices

Unless the business has approved pricing rules, the system should avoid giving exact quotes.

Safe wording:

> The team will review the details and confirm pricing after they understand the job properly.

---

## 16.2 The System Should Not Accept Every Job Automatically

Some jobs may be outside the service area, too small, too urgent, too risky, or not suitable.

The system should allow manual review.

---

## 16.3 The System Should Not Over-Message Customers

Follow-up should be helpful, not annoying.

Recommended rule:

- 2–4 follow-ups is usually enough for most new enquiries
- Quote follow-up should be polite
- Stop follow-up when customer replies or opts out

---

## 16.4 The System Should Not Replace Human Judgement

The system should support the business, but the owner or team should make important decisions.

---

## 16.5 The System Should Not Be Sold As Magic

The system improves enquiry handling and follow-up. It does not guarantee that every customer will book or that every campaign will perform perfectly.

# 18. Product Summary

The Tradie Front Desk System is a practical, managed system that helps trade businesses handle job enquiries better.

It is built around the mechanism:

# **Capture → Respond → Qualify → Book → Follow Up**

The system captures enquiries from calls, forms, missed calls, campaigns, and messages. It responds quickly, collects job details, moves suitable enquiries toward a quote or callback, follows up automatically, and tracks opportunities in a simple pipeline.

The product is designed for trade business owners who are busy, non-technical, and tired of chasing leads manually.

It should be sold as a front-desk system, not as AI, ads, CRM, or automation.

The customer-facing promise is:

# **More Booked Jobs. Less Chasing. No Tech Headaches.**

The internal delivery promise is:

> Build, test, launch, and manage a system that helps the business capture, respond to, follow up with, and organise job enquiries more effectively.

This document should guide all future system builds, website copy, sales calls, onboarding, and delivery SOPs.
