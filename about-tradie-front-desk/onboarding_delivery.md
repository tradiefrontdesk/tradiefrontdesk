- Client onboarding

- Launch
- Client training
- Monthly management
- Support boundaries

# 2. Delivery Philosophy

## 2.1 The Client Bought A Managed System

The client did not buy software.

They bought a managed front-desk system that helps capture enquiries, respond quickly, follow up, and organise job opportunities.

Therefore, delivery must feel like a service, not a login.

Bad delivery experience:

> Here is your CRM login. Watch these videos and set it up.

Good delivery experience:

> We will collect your business rules, build the system, test it, launch it, and show you only what you need to know.

---

## 2.2 The Client Should Not Need To Be Technical

Most trade business owners are busy and non-technical.

They do not need to understand:

- Automations
- Workflows
- API connections
- CRM settings
- AI prompts
- Webhooks
- Tags
- Trigger logic
- Campaign configuration

They only need to understand:

- What happens when an enquiry comes in
- How they are notified
- What they need to do next
- How to view opportunities if needed
- How to request a change
- How to mark a job as won/lost if required

---

## 2.3 The System Must Be Built Around The Business

Every trade business has different:

- Services
- Service areas
- Job values
- Urgency rules
- Good-fit jobs
- Bad-fit jobs
- Customer expectations
- Quote process
- Team structure
- Communication style
- Availability

The system should never be treated as a generic one-size-fits-all install.

The framework is standard.

The details are customised.

---

## 2.4 Build The Core First

Do not try to build everything at once.

The delivery priority is:

1. Capture enquiries
2. Respond instantly
3. Notify owner/team
4. Track in pipeline
5. Follow up
6. Add booking/callback
7. Add quote follow-up
8. Add reviews/reactivation/campaigns if included

The system should be launched in stages if needed.

This reduces risk and complexity.

---

## 2.5 Keep The Client In Control

The system should not make risky decisions without approval.

The client should control:

- Job types accepted
- Service areas
- Urgent job rules
- Pricing communication
- Whether bookings are automatic or request-based
- Whether AI voice is used
- When human handover happens
- Who receives notifications
- Which messages customers receive

# 3. Delivery Stages Overview

The full delivery process has ten stages.

## Stage 1: Sale Confirmation

Confirm package, scope, pricing, payment, and next steps.

## Stage 2: Onboarding Form

Collect business details, services, job rules, contact preferences, and system requirements.

## Stage 3: Access Collection

Collect access to required platforms and channels.

## Stage 4: System Strategy

Map the client’s enquiry journey and define the front-desk workflow.

## Stage 5: System Build

Build forms, pipeline, automations, messages, notifications, and included modules.

## Stage 6: Internal Testing

Test the system before showing it to the client.

## Stage 7: Client Review

Review scripts, workflows, notifications, and customer experience with the client.

## Stage 8: Launch

Turn the system on for real enquiries.

## Stage 9: Launch Monitoring

Monitor early activity and fix issues.

## Stage 10: Monthly Management

Review, optimise, support, and improve the system.

# 10. Onboarding Form: Good-Fit Jobs

## 10.1 Purpose

The system must understand what jobs the client wants more of.

## 10.2 Questions

- What does a perfect job look like for your business?
- What job types are most profitable?
- What job types are easiest to schedule?
- What job types lead to repeat work?
- What locations are best?
- What customer types are best?
- What urgency level is ideal?
- What minimum job value makes sense?

## 10.3 Good-Fit Criteria

Possible criteria:

- In service area
- Correct service type
- Property owner or decision maker
- Job value above threshold
- Realistic timeline
- Photos provided
- Clear problem description
- Customer contactable
- Not just price shopping

## 10.4 Example Good-Fit Definition

> A good-fit enquiry is a homeowner in West Auckland needing roof repair, replacement, or leak inspection, who can provide photos, wants work within the next 30 days, and is available for a callback.

# 11. Onboarding Form: Bad-Fit Jobs

## 11.1 Purpose

The system should help reduce time wasted on unsuitable enquiries.

## 11.2 Questions

- What jobs do you not want?
- What areas do you avoid?
- What job sizes are too small?
- What services do people ask for that you do not provide?
- Are there customer types you avoid?
- Are there urgency types you cannot handle?
- Should bad-fit enquiries be politely declined or sent to manual review?

## 11.3 Bad-Fit Criteria

Possible bad-fit criteria:

- Outside service area
- Wrong service type
- Too small
- Unrealistic timeframe
- No decision maker
- No budget alignment
- Unsafe work
- Jobs requiring unavailable licensing
- Emergency work not offered
- Commercial work if residential only
- Residential work if commercial only

## 11.4 Polite Decline Message

> Hi {{first_name}}, thanks for reaching out. Based on the details provided, this may not be the right fit for our services at the moment. We appreciate you contacting us and wish you all the best with the job.

Use only when client approves automated decline.

Default safer option:

> Flag for owner review.

# 12. Onboarding Form: Urgency Rules

## 12.1 Purpose

Urgent enquiries need special handling.

## 12.2 Questions

- Do you offer emergency work?
- What counts as urgent?
- What should happen for urgent enquiries?
- Who should be notified?
- Should urgent calls bypass normal follow-up?
- What hours are urgent jobs accepted?
- Are urgent jobs priced differently?

## 12.3 Urgency Categories

### Emergency

Requires immediate attention.

Examples:

- Burst pipe
- Electrical hazard
- Roof leak during storm
- No heating in winter
- Drain blockage

### Urgent

Needs attention within 24–48 hours.

### Normal

Can be scheduled.

### Future Work

Customer is planning ahead.

## 12.4 Urgency Routing Rules

Possible rules:

- Emergency: send immediate owner SMS and call notification
- Urgent: send owner SMS and move to urgent pipeline stage
- Normal: continue standard qualification
- Future: add to normal follow-up and booking flow

# 15. Onboarding Form: Booking And Callback Preferences

## 15.1 Questions

- Do you want customers to book directly?
- Do you prefer callback requests?
- Do you need to review enquiries before booking?
- Who should call the customer back?
- What times are available for callbacks?
- What times should not be offered?
- Do appointments need travel-time buffers?

## 15.2 Default Recommendation

For many tradies, do not start with fully automatic job booking.

# 16. Onboarding Form: Follow-Up Preferences

## 16.1 Questions

- How many times should new enquiries be followed up?
- How soon should the first follow-up happen?
- Should follow-up stop when a customer replies?
- Should quotes be followed up?
- How many days after quote should follow-up happen?
- Should old leads be reactivated?
- Should follow-ups be SMS, email, WhatsApp, or mixed?
- Should follow-up feel direct or soft?

## 16.2 Default Follow-Up Recommendation

For new enquiries:

- Immediate acknowledgement
- 1-hour follow-up if no reply
- Next-day follow-up
- 3-day final follow-up

For quotes:

- 24-hour check-in
- 3-day follow-up
- 7-day final follow-up

Adjust based on the client’s sales cycle.

# 22. Stage 6: Internal Testing

## 22.1 Purpose

Testing ensures the system works before real customers use it.

Never skip testing.

---

## 22.2 Testing Checklist

### Form Testing

- Submit test quote form
- Confirm contact is created
- Confirm opportunity is created
- Confirm correct pipeline stage
- Confirm customer receives message
- Confirm owner receives notification
- Confirm custom fields are captured

### Missed Call Testing

- Trigger missed call
- Confirm SMS sends
- Confirm contact is created/updated
- Confirm tag is applied
- Confirm owner notification sends
- Confirm follow-up starts if no reply

### Follow-Up Testing

- Confirm follow-up timing
- Confirm customer reply stops follow-up
- Confirm wrong follow-up does not continue
- Confirm no duplicate messages

### Pipeline Testing

- Move lead through stages
- Confirm correct automations trigger
- Confirm quote follow-up starts only at correct stage
- Confirm won/lost stops active follow-up

### Notification Testing

- Confirm owner receives notifications
- Confirm urgent notification works
- Confirm messages include useful details
- Confirm notifications are not excessive

### Booking/Callback Testing

- Submit callback request
- Confirm confirmation message
- Confirm owner notification
- Confirm calendar or task creation

### Review Testing

- Trigger review request
- Confirm correct message
- Confirm review link works
- Confirm only suitable customers receive request

### AI Phone Testing, If Included

- Test greeting
- Test service questions
- Test location capture
- Test urgency handling
- Test escalation
- Test no-pricing rule
- Test handover
- Test transcript/summary

---

## 22.3 Testing Rules

- Test every workflow before launch
- Use internal test contacts first
- Use client test contact before launch
- Document issues found
- Fix issues before launch
- Retest after changes
- Do not launch untested customer-facing flows

# 31. Common Delivery Issues And Fixes

## Issue 1: Client Delays Access

### Problem

Build cannot continue.

### Response

> We are ready to continue the build, but we still need access to {{required_access}}. Once that is provided, we can complete the next stage.

---

## Issue 2: Client Does Not Approve Messages

### Problem

Customer-facing launch is delayed.

### Response

> The system is built, but we need your approval on the customer messages before launch. This ensures the replies sound right for your business.

---

## Issue 3: Client Wants Extra Features Mid-Build

### Problem

Scope creep.

### Response

> That is a good idea. It is outside the current setup scope, so I suggest we launch the core front-desk system first and then quote this as an add-on once the main flow is live.

---

## Issue 4: Client Changes Services/Areas Often

### Problem

Workflow instability.

### Response

> We can update the system, but to keep it reliable, it is best to make service and area changes in batches rather than changing them constantly.

---

## Issue 5: Notifications Are Too Frequent

### Problem

Owner gets overwhelmed.

### Fix

- Reduce low-priority notifications
- Use urgent-only SMS
- Send summary emails
- Route only good-fit leads

---

## Issue 6: Customer Messages Are Too Strong Or Too Soft

### Fix

Adjust tone and follow-up timing.

---

## Issue 7: Leads Are Poor Fit

### Fix

- Add qualification questions
- Add service area filter
- Add job type filter
- Add manual review stage
- Adjust campaign targeting if campaigns included
