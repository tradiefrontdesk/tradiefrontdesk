# 1. Purpose Of This Document

This document defines the ideal customer for **The Tradie Front Desk System**.

It explains who the system is for, who it is not for, what the buyer cares about, what problems they are aware of, what problems they are not aware of, what they fear, what they desire, and what language should be used to sell to them.

This document should guide:

- Website copy
- Sales pages
- Sales scripts
- Ad copy
- Outreach messages
- Email campaigns
- Webinar scripts
- Lead magnet creation
- Front Desk Audit questions
- Objection handling
- Onboarding questions
- Product packaging
- Pricing strategy
- Niche selection

The goal is to avoid selling from the perspective of technology and instead sell from the perspective of the trade business owner’s daily reality.

The customer does not wake up wanting:

- AI agents
- CRM pipelines
- Automations
- Meta ads
- Google lead forms
- Voice bots
- Software dashboards
- Workflow systems

The customer wakes up wanting:

- More good jobs
- Less admin
- Less stress
- Fewer missed calls
- Faster follow-up
- Better organisation
- Less chasing
- More control
- A business that does not depend on them personally answering every enquiry instantly

This document keeps all messaging focused on that reality.

# 2. Primary Customer Avatar

## 2.1 Avatar Name

# **The Busy Trade Business Owner**

This is the primary buyer.

They own or manage a trade business and are still involved in daily operations.

They may be on the tools, quoting jobs, managing staff, taking calls, replying to customers, and trying to grow the business at the same time.

They are not looking for complicated marketing systems. They are looking for practical help that produces better job opportunities and reduces the chaos around enquiries.

---

## 2.2 Basic Profile

### Business Type

Trade or home service business.

### Typical Business Size

Best fit:

- 3–20 staff
- Owner-led
- Small team with growth potential
- Enough work to be busy, but not enough systems to be organised

Secondary fit:

- Solo operator with high-value jobs and growth ambition
- Larger trade business with poor enquiry handling

### Location

Can work in New Zealand, Australia, UK, US, Canada, or similar local-service markets.

Initial ideal market:

- New Zealand and Australia trade businesses

### Role Of Buyer

Usually:

- Owner
- Founder
- Managing director
- Operations manager
- Office manager in larger trade business
- Business partner/spouse handling admin

### Technical Ability

Usually low to moderate.

They can use:

- Phone
- Email
- Facebook
- Google Business Profile
- Basic apps
- Text messages
- Maybe Xero, Tradify, ServiceM8, Fergus, Simpro, or similar tools

They may not be comfortable with:

- CRM automation
- Ad platforms
- Funnels
- AI tools
- Integrations
- Tracking
- Analytics
- Complex dashboards

### Buying Power

They can usually spend money if the value is clear.

They may resist marketing spend if they have been burned before, but they will invest in practical systems that feel directly connected to revenue or time savings.

# 4. Poor-Fit Customers

## 4.1 Bad-Fit Customer Types

The system should avoid customers who are likely to be difficult, unprofitable, or unsuccessful.

Bad-fit customers include:

- Brand-new businesses with no money
- Businesses with no capacity to take more work
- Businesses that expect guaranteed jobs with no participation
- Businesses with very low job values
- Businesses with poor service quality
- Businesses with terrible reviews and no desire to improve
- Owners who refuse to respond to opportunities
- Owners who want everything for free
- Businesses that only want cheap leads
- Owners who blame every problem on marketing
- Businesses with unclear services or messy internal operations
- Businesses that do not answer calls at all and refuse to change
- Businesses that cannot provide basic access or information
- Businesses that want complicated customisation but will not pay for it

---

## 4.2 Warning Signs During Sales

Watch for prospects who say:

- “Can you guarantee jobs?”
- “I do not want to do anything.”
- “I tried everything and nothing works.”
- “My customers are all idiots.”
- “I do not care about reviews.”
- “I do not want to reply to leads.”
- “I do not want to pay setup.”
- “Just send me leads.”
- “I want results before paying anything.”
- “I am too busy to give you business details.”
- “I want the cheapest option.”
- “I do not want any monthly fee.”

Not every one of these is an automatic rejection, but they are red flags.

---

## 4.3 Bad-Fit Business Conditions

The system is less suitable if:

- Average job value is too low
- Business has no margin
- Owner is not willing to improve follow-up
- Lead volume is too small to matter
- Owner cannot define good-fit jobs
- Service area is too limited
- Business reputation is poor
- No one will act on qualified opportunities
- Customer service is weak
- Business is not legally or operationally ready

# 15. Sales Psychology

## 15.1 The Buyer Wants Gain And Pain Relief

The buyer wants both:

### Gain

- More booked jobs
- Better enquiries
- More organised business
- More reviews
- More control

### Pain Relief

- Less chasing
- Less admin
- Fewer missed calls
- Less stress
- Less confusion

The best message includes both:

> More booked jobs. Less chasing. No tech headaches.

---

## 15.2 The Buyer Responds To Specificity

Do not say:

> We automate your business.

Say:

> If you miss a call, the system texts the customer back. If they submit a quote request, it replies instantly. If they do not respond, it follows up. If a quote is sent, it reminds them.

Specificity builds trust.

---

## 15.3 The Buyer Hates Feeling Sold To

Avoid hype.

Do not say:

- Explode your business
- 10X your trade company overnight
- AI will replace your staff
- Guaranteed unlimited leads
- Never lose a job again

Use grounded language:

- Helps reduce missed opportunities
- Helps follow up faster
- Helps organise enquiries
- Helps turn more quote requests into booked jobs

---

## 15.4 The Buyer Wants To Feel Understood

The strongest reaction is:

> These people get it.

Use lines like:

- You are busy on the tools.
- You cannot always answer the phone.
- You should not have to chase every lead manually.
- Your enquiries should not live across texts, emails, missed calls, and memory.
- Before you spend more on leads, fix the process that handles them.

# 16. Therefore, So What Analysis

This section applies the full problem-solving technique to the avatar.

---

## 16.1 They Are Tradies

They are tradies.

**So what?**

They spend much of the day working physically, driving, quoting, managing jobs, and solving customer problems.

**Therefore?**

They cannot consistently sit at a desk answering enquiries and managing follow-up.

**So what?**

They miss calls, reply late, and forget some opportunities.

**Therefore?**

They need a system that handles the first response and follow-up.

**So what?**

The offer should be positioned as a front desk, not software.

**Therefore?**

The best message is: “Your trade business needs a front desk system, not more admin.”

---

## 16.2 They Are Busy

They are busy.

**So what?**

They do not want more tasks.

**Therefore?**

The system must be done-for-you.

**So what?**

The sales message must remove effort.

**Therefore?**

Say: “We set it up and manage it for you.”

**So what?**

This reduces resistance and makes the system feel easy to adopt.

---

## 16.3 They Are Not Technical

They are not usually technical.

**So what?**

Tech language creates friction.

**Therefore?**

Avoid words like CRM, automation stack, workflow, AI agent, integration, and funnel in first-touch messaging.

**So what?**

Use plain language: calls, enquiries, follow-up, quote requests, booked jobs.

**Therefore?**

The system becomes understandable and less intimidating.

---

## 16.4 They Want Jobs, Not Leads

They want jobs, not leads.

**So what?**

Selling leads can feel weak or untrustworthy.

**Therefore?**

Sell booked quote requests and better enquiry handling.

**So what?**

The offer should not say “we generate leads” as the main promise.

**Therefore?**

Say: “We help turn enquiries into booked jobs.”

---

## 16.5 They Worry About Ads

They worry about ads.

**So what?**

If we lead with ads, we trigger fear of wasted spend.

**Therefore?**

Ads should be optional and positioned as a growth layer.

**So what?**

The front-desk system can be sold before campaign management.

**Therefore?**

The business avoids sounding like a full SMMA.

---

## 16.6 They Distrust Agencies

They may distrust agencies.

**So what?**

They have heard big promises before.

**Therefore?**

We must be specific, practical, and clear.

**So what?**

Show the actual system flow.

**Therefore?**

Trust comes from transparency, not hype.

---

## 16.7 They Fear AI

They may fear AI.

**So what?**

Leading with AI could create objections.

**Therefore?**

Do not make AI the hero.

**So what?**

Use AI behind the scenes where useful, but sell the front-desk outcome.

**Therefore?**

The offer feels safer and more practical.

---

## 16.8 They Need Control

They need control.

**So what?**

They do not want a system accepting bad jobs or making promises.

**Therefore?**

The system must allow owner review and approval.

**So what?**

Direct booking may not always be suitable.

**Therefore?**

Use callback requests, quote requests, and manual approval where needed.

---

## 16.9 They Are Proud Of Their Work

They are proud of their trade.

**So what?**

They do not want to feel like they are bad at business.

**Therefore?**

The message should not shame them.

**So what?**

Say: “You are busy doing the work. The system handles the first response.”

**Therefore?**

The offer feels supportive, not insulting.

---

## 16.10 They Want Simplicity

They want simplicity.

**So what?**

Too many features confuse them.

**Therefore?**

The product should be explained in one simple flow.

**So what?**

Repeat: Capture, Respond, Qualify, Book, Follow Up.

**Therefore?**

The buyer can understand and remember the offer.

- You are on-site and miss a call
- A website enquiry arrives after hours

- A quote is sent and never followed up

- A generic AI platform
- A marketing agency
- A tech startup

# 21. Final Customer Avatar Summary

The ideal customer is a busy trade business owner who wants more booked jobs, better enquiry handling, and less manual chasing without learning software, running ads, or trusting random lead sellers.

They are likely busy, practical, skeptical, and time-poor.

They do not want to be sold AI or automation as a concept. They want a simple business improvement:

> When someone enquires, the business replies fast, follows up, and moves the customer toward a booked quote or job.

The strongest emotional promise is:

> You can stay focused on the work while your front-desk system catches, replies, follows up, and keeps opportunities moving.

The strongest practical promise is:

> Every enquiry is captured, responded to, followed up, and organised.

The strongest sales message is:

# **More Booked Jobs. Less Chasing. No Tech Headaches.**

The strongest buyer insight is:

> Most tradies do not need another app, another agency, or another lead seller. They need a simple front-desk system that helps stop good jobs slipping through the cracks.
