# 1. Purpose Of This Document

This document defines the **Front Desk Audit** for The Tradie Front Desk System.

The audit is designed to be the main front-end offer, diagnostic tool, lead magnet, and sales conversation framework.

The purpose of the audit is to help a trade business owner clearly see where job opportunities may be leaking from their current enquiry process.

The audit should not feel like a generic marketing consultation.

It should feel like a practical business checkup.

The audit should answer questions like:

- What happens when a customer calls and no one answers?
- What happens when someone fills in the website form?
- How fast does the business reply?
- Are quotes being followed up?
- Are old leads being reactivated?
- Are enquiries being tracked in one place?
- Does the owner know what needs attention?
- Are customers given a clear next step?
- Is the business ready for more enquiries?

The audit creates the bridge between the prospect’s current problem and the paid solution.

The paid solution is:

# **The Tradie Front Desk System**

The audit should make the prospect think:

> I can see where opportunities are being lost. I need a better system for this.

---

# 2. Strategic Role Of The Audit

## 2.1 Why The Audit Exists

Many trade business owners do not realise they have an enquiry-handling problem.

They may think they need:

- More leads
- Better ads
- A new website
- More referrals
- Cheaper marketing
- A receptionist
- A CRM

But often the first problem is simpler:

> Existing enquiries are not being captured, responded to, followed up, or organised properly.

The audit reveals this problem.

---

## 2.2 What The Audit Should Sell

The audit should not immediately sell ads.

The audit should sell the idea that the business needs a better front-desk system.

The audit should educate the prospect that there are two parts to growth:

1. Getting enquiries
2. Handling enquiries properly

Most businesses focus on getting more enquiries, but they lose money because the second part is weak.

Audit positioning line:

> Before you spend more money getting enquiries, make sure your business can capture, respond to, and follow up with the enquiries it already receives.

---

## 2.3 What The Audit Should Not Be

The audit should not be:

- A generic marketing review
- A website roast
- A technical CRM demo
- A sales pitch disguised as an audit
- A full free strategy project
- A detailed free implementation plan
- A promise of guaranteed results
- A deep operations consulting session
- A complicated report the owner will not read

The audit should be simple, direct, visual, and focused on enquiry leaks.

At the end of the audit, the prospect should receive a simple diagnosis in three parts:

## 4.1 What Is Working

The strengths in their current enquiry process.

Examples:

- They have a website form
- They answer most calls
- They have good reviews
- They get regular referrals
- They have strong quote demand
- They have a service area that can support growth

Where opportunities may be lost.

Examples:

- No follow-up sequence
- Quotes are not followed up consistently
- Old leads are not reactivated

# 5. Audit Philosophy

## 5.1 The Audit Must Be Practical

Trade owners do not want a 40-page theoretical report.

They want to know:

- What is broken?
- What is costing me opportunities?
- What should I fix first?
- How hard is it to fix?
- What is the value of fixing it?

---

## 5.2 The Audit Must Avoid Shame

Do not make the owner feel stupid.

Bad wording:

> Your business is losing jobs because your systems are terrible.

Better wording:

> This is common for busy trade businesses. The owner is usually on the tools, so enquiries often end up scattered across calls, texts, emails, and memory.

---

## 5.3 The Audit Must Create Clarity

The audit should make the problem obvious.

The prospect should understand:

- Where enquiries come from
- What happens after they arrive
- Where delays happen
- What is missing
- What the next step should be

---

## 5.4 The Audit Must Lead Naturally To The Offer

The audit should not force the sale.

It should make the paid system feel like the logical fix.

Example:

Audit finding:

> Missed calls are not being automatically followed up.

Logical solution:

> Missed Call Saver module.

Audit finding:

> Quote requests go to email and are manually followed up.

Logical solution:

> Instant Enquiry Response + Follow-Up Engine + Job Pipeline.

# 6. Audit Scoring System

The audit should include a simple score out of 100.

This gives the prospect a clear benchmark.

## 6.1 Total Score

# **Front Desk Score: /100**

Recommended score categories:

1. Call Handling — 15 points
2. Missed Call Recovery — 10 points
3. Website Enquiry Capture — 15 points
4. Speed To Response — 10 points
5. Lead Qualification — 10 points
6. Booking / Callback Process — 10 points
7. Follow-Up System — 15 points
8. Pipeline Visibility — 10 points
9. Reputation / Review Flow — 5 points

Total: 100 points

---

## 6.2 Score Interpretation

### 0–30: High Leak Risk

The business is likely losing a significant number of opportunities through slow replies, missed calls, poor capture, or lack of follow-up.

Message:

> Your enquiry process has several major leaks. Before spending more on lead generation, the priority should be installing a basic front-desk system.

---

### 31–50: Weak Front Desk

The business has some enquiry handling in place, but the process is inconsistent and likely depends heavily on the owner’s memory.

Message:

> You have some pieces in place, but there are clear gaps in response, follow-up, and tracking.

---

### 51–70: Functional But Leaking

The business has a working process, but opportunities may still be slipping through due to missed calls, manual follow-up, or scattered tracking.

Message:

> Your front-desk process is working in parts, but the gaps may still cost jobs when you get busy.

---

### 71–85: Strong But Improveable

The business is doing better than most but could still benefit from automation, tracking, or better follow-up.

Message:

> You have a strong base. The next step is improving consistency and reducing manual effort.

---

### 86–100: Excellent Front Desk

The business has strong enquiry handling, fast response, follow-up, tracking, and customer communication.

Message:

> Your enquiry process is already strong. You may not need the core system, but growth campaigns, reactivation, or advanced optimisation may still be relevant.

# 7. Audit Category 1: Call Handling

## Points

15 points

## Purpose

Assess what happens when a customer calls the business.

## Key Questions

- Who answers incoming calls?
- Are calls answered during work hours?
- What happens if the owner is on-site and cannot answer?
- Is there voicemail?
- Is voicemail checked regularly?
- Are calls logged anywhere?
- Are calls assigned to a person?
- Are missed calls followed up?
- Are urgent calls handled differently?

## Scoring

### 0 Points

No clear call handling process. Calls are answered when possible and forgotten when missed.

### 5 Points

Calls are usually answered, but missed calls are handled manually and inconsistently.

### 10 Points

Calls are answered consistently and missed calls are usually reviewed or called back manually.

### 15 Points

Calls are answered or captured consistently, missed calls trigger a process, and urgent calls are clearly handled.

## Red Flags

- Owner misses calls while on-site
- Missed calls are not texted back
- Voicemail is not checked
- No record of missed calls
- Customers call multiple times
- No urgent job process

## Recommended Fixes

- Missed Call Saver
- Owner notification system
- Urgent job routing
- AI phone assistant if suitable

## Sales Angle

> Missed calls should not mean missed jobs.

---

# 8. Audit Category 2: Missed Call Recovery

10 points

Assess whether missed callers are automatically or reliably re-engaged.

## Key Questions

- Does the business send an automatic text after a missed call?
- Is the missed caller asked what they need help with?
- Is the customer added to a pipeline?
- Is the owner notified?
- Is there a follow-up if the customer does not respond?
- Are missed calls tracked over time?

## Scoring

### 0 Points

No missed-call recovery process.

### 3 Points

Owner manually calls back when they remember.

### 6 Points

Most missed calls are manually followed up, but there is no automation or tracking.

### 10 Points

Missed calls trigger an instant text, are logged, and are followed up if needed.

## Red Flags

- Missed calls go unanswered for hours
- No automatic reply
- No missed call tracking
- No follow-up if the customer does not answer
- Owner assumes they will remember to call back

## Recommended Fixes

- Missed Call Saver
- Missed call follow-up sequence
- Customer database tagging
- Owner notification

## Sales Angle

> If you cannot answer, the system keeps the customer warm until you can.

# 20. Audit Sales Call Structure

## 20.1 Call Goal

The goal of the audit call is to diagnose the enquiry process and recommend the right system.

The goal is not to pitch too early.

---

## 20.2 Call Flow

### Step 1: Set Context

> The goal today is to look at what happens when someone contacts your business — calls, forms, missed calls, quotes, follow-up — and identify where jobs might be slipping through.

---

### Step 2: Understand Business

Ask:

- What services do you offer?
- What jobs are most valuable?
- What areas do you service?
- Are you trying to grow right now?

---

### Step 3: Map Current Enquiry Journey

Ask:

> Walk me through what happens when someone calls or asks for a quote.

Listen for:

- Delay
- Manual handling
- No follow-up
- Scattered systems
- Owner overload

---

### Step 4: Identify Leaks

Point out gaps gently.

Example:

> So if someone calls while you are on-site and you miss it, there is no automatic reply or follow-up. That means the customer may call someone else before you get back to them.

---

### Step 5: Quantify Value

Ask:

- What is an average good job worth?
- How many calls do you think you miss per week?
- How many quotes do you send per month?
- How many quotes go unfollowed?
- What would one extra booked job per month be worth?

---

### Step 6: Present Diagnosis

Use this format:

> From what you have told me, your biggest leaks are likely missed-call recovery, quote follow-up, and pipeline visibility.

---

### Step 7: Present Solution

Explain the system only after the problem is clear.

> The way we would fix this is by installing a front-desk system that catches missed calls, replies to enquiries instantly, collects job details, follows up, and keeps everything in a simple pipeline.

---

### Step 8: Recommend Package

Example:

> I would not start you with the full managed campaign package yet. First, I would install the Booked Jobs System because you already get enquiries. The priority is capturing and converting them better.
