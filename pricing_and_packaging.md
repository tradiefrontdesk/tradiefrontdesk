# 1. Purpose Of This Document

This document defines the pricing and packaging strategy for **The Tradie Front Desk System**.

It is designed to become the commercial source of truth for:

- Sales calls
- Proposals
- Website pricing pages
- Internal quoting
- Package recommendations
- Scope control
- Upsells
- Add-ons
- Renewals
- Client onboarding
- Invoicing
- Offer testing
- Value positioning
- Pricing objection handling

The goal is to create a pricing model that feels clear, valuable, practical, and easy for a trade business owner to understand.

This business should not sell itself like:

- A cheap software subscription
- A pay-per-lead agency
- A generic ad agency
- A one-off automation build
- A freelancer doing random GoHighLevel setups
- A complex AI implementation service

It should sell itself like:

> A managed front-desk system that helps trade businesses capture enquiries, respond faster, follow up consistently, and turn more quote requests into booked jobs.

The price should be framed against:

- Missed jobs
- Lost quotes
- Poor follow-up
- Hiring admin
- Wasted ad spend
- Shared lead dependency
- Time spent chasing customers
- Enquiries slipping through the cracks

Not against:

- Cheap CRM subscriptions
- Low-cost website forms
- DIY automation tools
- Generic chatbot widgets

# 2. Pricing Philosophy

## 2.1 Sell A Managed Business System, Not Software

The client is not paying for access to GoHighLevel.

The client is paying for:

- System strategy
- Setup
- Configuration
- Message writing
- Pipeline setup
- Automation build
- Testing
- Launch
- Monitoring
- Support
- Ongoing management
- Optimisation
- Business-specific workflow design

Therefore, the pricing must reflect service value, not platform cost.

Bad pricing frame:

> This includes a CRM and automations.

Better pricing frame:

> This gives your business a managed front-desk system that captures enquiries, replies fast, follows up, and keeps job opportunities organised.

---

## 2.2 Do Not Compete With Cheap Lead Sellers

Lead sellers often use simple promises:

- “We send you leads.”
- “Pay per lead.”
- “Guaranteed leads.”
- “Exclusive leads.”

The Tradie Front Desk System is different.

It should not be priced like a lead broker.

It should be priced like infrastructure.

Positioning:

> Lead sellers give you names and numbers. We build the system that helps your business capture, follow up, and convert its own opportunities.

---

## 2.3 Do Not Lead With Ad Management

The offer may include ads as an optional growth layer, but the core identity is not advertising.

Why?

Because many tradies worry about:

- Running ads themselves
- Wasting ad spend
- Paying agencies without seeing jobs
- Getting poor-quality leads
- Having more admin to manage

Therefore, ads should be positioned as:

> A growth layer added after the front-desk system is ready.

Not:

> The main product.

---

## 2.4 Setup Fee And Monthly Fee Must Be Separate

The business should charge both:

1. **Setup fee** — for building and launching the system
2. **Monthly fee** — for keeping the system live, managed, supported, and improved

This is important because setup is real work and monthly support is ongoing value.

Do not hide setup inside the monthly fee unless strategically needed.

---

## 2.5 Price Should Increase With Complexity And Outcome Potential

The price should depend on:

- Number of enquiry sources
- Number of services
- Number of locations/service areas
- Follow-up complexity
- Whether campaigns are included
- Whether AI phone is included
- Whether reactivation is included
- Whether review automation is included
- Whether website work is included
- Whether integrations are included
- How much monthly management is required
- Potential job value for the business

A roofing company doing high-value roof replacements can justify higher pricing than a low-margin handyman with small jobs.

# 10. Value Anchoring

## 10.1 Anchor Against Missed Jobs

Ask:

> What is one good job worth to you?

Then frame:

> If one good job is worth $3,000–$10,000, the system does not need to recover many missed opportunities to justify itself.

---

## 10.2 Anchor Against Hiring Admin

Frame:

> Hiring a part-time or full-time admin person can cost thousands per month, and they still need a system. This gives your business the front-desk process before or alongside hiring.

---

## 10.3 Anchor Against Wasted Ad Spend

Frame:

> If you spend money on ads but calls are missed and follow-up is weak, ad spend gets wasted. The front-desk system helps make sure enquiries are handled properly first.

---

## 10.4 Anchor Against Lead Sellers

Frame:

> Buying leads gives you names and numbers. This builds the system that helps you capture, follow up, and own your pipeline.

---

## 10.5 Anchor Against Owner Time

Frame:

> The owner’s time is expensive. If the system removes chasing, remembering, and manual follow-up, it frees the owner to focus on higher-value work.

# 12. Discount And Negotiation Rules

## 12.1 Do Not Discount First

Do not offer a discount before the prospect asks.

Discounting too early weakens value.

---

## 12.2 Reduce Scope Before Reducing Price

If they cannot afford the recommended package, reduce scope.

Example:

Instead of discounting Booked Jobs System, offer Front Desk Starter.

Script:

> We can reduce the scope and start with the highest-priority leaks first, but I would not want to cut the parts that make the system work.

---

## 12.3 Acceptable Discount Options

If needed, use value-preserving discounts:

### Option 1: Pay Setup In Full Discount

> Pay setup upfront and receive a small reduction.

### Option 2: Longer Minimum Term Discount

> Lower monthly fee for a longer commitment.

### Option 3: Founding Client Rate

> Limited early-client pricing in exchange for feedback/testimonial.

### Option 4: Remove Add-Ons

> Remove campaign/review/reactivation modules.

---

## 12.4 Avoid Bad Discounts

Avoid:

- Random 50% discounts
- Pay-after-results offers
- Free setup for unqualified clients
- No monthly fee
- Unlimited work included
- Discounting because they compare you to software

---

## 12.5 Founding Client Pricing Rule

If offering early pricing, make it clear it is conditional.

Example:

> Because we are building early case studies in this niche, we can offer a founding client rate for the first three clients. In return, we ask for feedback, permission to use anonymised results, and a testimonial if the system is useful.

### Setup Fee

Starts either:

Cancellation should be in writing.

---

## 13.4 Late Payment

- Grace period: 3–7 days
- Service may pause after non-payment
- Reactivation fee may apply after extended non-payment

Clear boundaries protect profit.

- Small message updates
- Small service/area updates

- Follow-up timing adjustments
- Notification recipient changes
- Monthly reporting

- Full SEO campaign
- Full social media management

- Manual outbound calling

- New campaign funnels outside scope
- Custom software development
- Large AI phone redesign

Small changes are included.

# 17. Pricing Objection Handling

---

## 17.1 “It Is Too Expensive”

Response:

> I understand. The important thing is not to compare it to software. Compare it to missed calls, lost quotes, poor follow-up, and the cost of hiring admin. If one good job is worth a few thousand dollars, the system does not need to recover many opportunities to make sense.

---

## 17.2 “Can You Do It Cheaper?”

Response:

> We can reduce the scope, but I would not want to remove the parts that make the system work. If budget is the main issue, we can start with the Front Desk Starter and focus on the highest-priority leaks first.

---

## 17.3 “Why Is There A Setup Fee?”

Response:

> The setup fee covers building the system around your business: services, locations, forms, messages, pipeline, missed-call flow, follow-up, owner notifications, testing, and launch. It is not just turning on software.

---

## 17.4 “Why Is There A Monthly Fee?”

Response:

> The monthly fee keeps the system live, managed, monitored, supported, and improved. It covers the platform, workflow checks, message updates, reporting, support, and optimisation so you are not left alone with software.

---

## 17.5 “Can I Just Pay Per Lead?”

Response:

> That is not really what this is. Pay-per-lead models usually keep you dependent on the lead source. This system is about building your own enquiry pipeline and follow-up process inside your business.

---

## 17.6 “Can I Pay After Results?”

Response:

> We do not do pay-after-results because the system requires real setup, build, testing, and management work upfront. We also do not control every factor that affects job outcomes, such as pricing, availability, market demand, and closing. What we guarantee is the agreed system build and launch.

---

## 17.7 “I Need To Think About It”

Response:

> Of course. What part do you mainly need to think about — the price, the timing, whether the system fits, or whether it will work for your business?

# 21. Profitability Considerations

## 21.1 Avoid Underpricing Setup

Setup can take significant time.

Tasks include:

- Discovery
- Strategy
- Forms
- Messages
- Workflows
- Pipeline
- Testing
- Review
- Launch

If setup is underpriced, delivery becomes stressful.

---

## 21.2 Avoid High-Touch Low-Monthly Clients

Low monthly clients can become unprofitable if support is not controlled.

For Starter clients, keep monthly support limited.

---

## 21.3 Control Add-Ons

Do not include too many add-ons in low-tier packages.

Each add-on creates:

- More setup
- More testing
- More support
- More risk
- More client questions

---

## 21.4 Campaigns Require Separate Margin

Ad management is ongoing work.

Do not include full ad management inside a low monthly fee.

---

## 21.5 AI Phone Can Create Usage Costs

If AI phone assistant is included, account for:

- AI usage costs
- Call costs
- Phone number costs
- Testing time
- Monitoring time
- Script adjustments

Do not include unlimited AI voice usage without metered pricing or fair use rules.
