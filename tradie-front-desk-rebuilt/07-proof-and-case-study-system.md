# Proof And Case Study System

## Proof Philosophy

The market is full of people promising tradies more leads, better ads, AI receptionists, websites, SEO, and guaranteed growth.

Tradie Front Desk should win trust with practical proof, not hype.

Proof should show:

- the mess before
- the system installed
- what changed
- what the business owner experienced
- what measurable activity happened
- what outcomes were supported

Avoid claiming the system caused every business result unless that can be proven.

Use safer phrasing:

- "helped"
- "supported"
- "contributed to"
- "created the system behind"
- "improved response and follow-up"
- "gave the owner visibility"

Avoid:

- "guaranteed"
- "caused all growth"
- "will get the same result"
- "never miss a job again"

## Proof Assets To Collect

### 1. Enquiry Capture Proof

Collect:

- number of new enquiries captured
- channels captured
- website form submissions
- missed calls recorded
- social messages routed
- Google Business messages routed
- campaign leads routed

Website use:

> "In the first month, the system captured [X] enquiries across calls, forms, and messages."

Proof note:

> This shows operational improvement, not guaranteed revenue.

### 2. Response Speed Proof

Collect:

- average response time before
- average response time after
- number of instant replies sent
- number of missed-call text-backs sent

Website use:

> "Missed callers received an automatic reply instead of silence."

### 3. Follow-Up Proof

Collect:

- no-response follow-ups sent
- quote follow-ups sent
- follow-up replies received
- opportunities revived

Website use:

> "The system followed up [X] quiet enquiries and [Y] old quotes."

### 4. Booking Proof

Collect:

- callbacks booked
- quote requests booked
- site visits booked
- calendar bookings created
- booking conversion from replies

Website use:

> "The business could see which enquiries had moved to callback, quote, or site visit."

High-value trade examples:

- roof inspection requests captured
- site inspections booked
- switchboard quote callbacks booked
- drainage site visits booked
- inspection-to-quote movement
- high-value quotes followed up

### 5. Reactivation Proof

Collect:

- old leads uploaded
- invalid contacts removed
- reactivation messages sent
- replies received
- interested replies
- callbacks booked
- jobs quoted

Website use:

> "Old leads were not treated as dead. They were re-contacted and routed into the pipeline."

### 6. Review Proof

Collect:

- review requests sent
- reviews gained
- star rating change
- review response examples

Website use:

> "Happy customers were asked for reviews automatically after completed jobs."

### 7. Operational Proof

Collect:

- pipeline screenshots
- message sequence screenshots
- missed-call workflow screenshot
- quote form screenshot
- audit scorecard screenshot
- booking calendar screenshot
- monthly report screenshot

Website use:

> Show the system visually so the owner understands what is being built without learning the technology.

## Case Study Structure

Use this structure for every case study.

### 1. Business Snapshot

Include:

- trade
- location
- team size
- job type
- average job value, if the client is comfortable sharing it
- main enquiry channels
- starting problem

Example:

> A 7-person plumbing business in Auckland getting calls, website forms, and repeat customer enquiries, but missing calls during the day and relying on manual quote follow-up.

### 2. The Problem

Describe the operational leak:

- missed calls
- slow replies
- no quote follow-up
- scattered messages
- no pipeline
- weak review process
- old leads untouched

Keep it plain:

> The owner was not short on effort. The problem was that enquiries were spread across too many places and follow-up depended on memory.

### 3. The System Installed

Describe buyer-facing components:

- missed-call text-back
- instant reply
- quote request form
- callback booking
- quote follow-up
- old lead reactivation
- review request flow
- pipeline

Avoid deep technical details.

### 4. What Changed

Show practical change:

- missed calls got a response
- new enquiries were organised
- quotes were followed up
- old leads were contacted
- reviews were requested
- owner had a clearer view

### 5. Metrics

Use only true metrics.

Possible metrics:

- enquiries captured
- missed-call replies sent
- follow-ups sent
- replies received
- callbacks booked
- quote requests booked
- reviews gained
- old leads reactivated
- average response time
- average job value, if available
- inspection-to-quote conversion, if applicable
- recovered quote value, if applicable

### 6. Owner Quote

Ask for a quote using prompts:

- What was happening before?
- What changed after the system went live?
- What did it save you from doing manually?
- What would you say to another tradie considering it?

### 7. Disclaimer

Add:

> Results depend on the business, market, offer, response speed, pricing, reputation, and follow-through. This case study is an example, not a guarantee.

Reduce risk before purchase.

Copy:

Purpose:

Make the audit feel real.

Show:

- sample Front Desk Scorecard
- sample leak findings

Copy:

## Front Desk Scorecard

Use a simple scorecard in audits and website visuals.

Categories:

- missed-call handling
- response speed
- website enquiry capture
- quote request clarity
- booking/callback path
- quote follow-up
- old lead database
- social message handling
- Google Business Profile message handling
- review request process
- pipeline visibility
- campaign lead speed-to-lead

Score each:

- green: working
- yellow: partial
- red: leaking

## Screenshot Guidance

Use screenshots that show practical outcomes, not complex technical setup.

Good screenshots:

- simple pipeline with stages
- missed-call reply example
- quote follow-up sequence
- audit scorecard
- booking calendar
- review request flow
- monthly report

Avoid screenshots that show:

- complicated workflow builders
- technical configuration
- API settings
- dense dashboards
- AI prompt engineering

## Proof Before Case Studies Exist

If strong trade case studies are not ready yet, use:

- sample audit screenshots
- anonymised workflow screenshots
- demo missed-call flow
- demo quote follow-up flow
- founder explanation
- founding-client offer
- transparent disclaimer

Do not fake proof.

Acceptable copy:

> We are currently building our trade-specific case study library. Until then, we will show you exactly what gets installed, what gets measured, and how the system works before you commit.

## Testimonial Questions

Ask clients:

- What was happening with calls, enquiries, or follow-up before?
- What changed after the system went live?
- What part saved you the most time?
- Did it help you look more professional to customers?
- What would have happened if you kept doing it manually?
- What would you tell another tradie considering Tradie Front Desk?

## Proof Rule

Every claim should connect to one of three things:

1. A real metric.
2. A real screenshot.
3. A real story.

If none exists, keep the claim modest.
