# Website Blueprint

## Website Goal

The website should convert qualified trade business owners into booked Free Front Desk Audits.

Primary CTA:

**Book a Free Front Desk Audit**

Secondary CTA:

**See How It Works**

The website should make a tradie think:

> These people understand how trade businesses lose jobs. This is not another lead seller, agency, or app. This is the front-desk system I need behind my calls, quotes, messages, and follow-up.

## Website Position

Hero-level message:

> Stop losing good jobs from missed calls, slow replies, and forgotten follow-up.

Subheadline:

> Tradie Front Desk installs and manages a front-desk system that captures enquiries, replies fast, follows up, and helps turn quote requests into booked jobs while you stay focused on the work.

Trust line:

> Not shared leads. Not just ads. Not another CRM. A managed enquiry system built for tradies.

## Global Design And Image Direction

Colours and detailed design can be decided later. The structure should still plan for strong visuals.

The website should include:

- real tradie owners and teams
- people on site, in vans, on phones, with tablets
- job-site imagery
- office/admin handoff imagery
- phone message screenshots
- simple before/after diagrams
- audit scorecard mockups
- pipeline screenshots
- booking page screenshots

Mandatory asset types by page:

- Home: trade owner image plus missed-call / quote-follow-up split visual
- Audit: scorecard, calendar, and leak report mockup
- Pricing: pricing cards and value anchor callout
- Proof: screenshots, monthly report, and before/after flow
- Trade pages: trade-specific contextual photography, not generic service imagery

Avoid:

- generic robot imagery
- abstract AI graphics
- stock call-centre operators with headsets
- complex dashboard screenshots above the fold
- agency-style laptop mockups with no trade context

## Page 2: How It Works

Goal:

Explain the system simply without technical jargon.

Sections:

1. Hero:
   - "How Tradie Front Desk Works"

2. Simple flow:
   - Capture -> Respond -> Qualify -> Book -> Follow Up

3. Capture:
   - calls, missed calls, forms, social, Google, old leads, campaign leads

4. Respond:
   - instant replies, missed-call text-back, after-hours response

5. Urgent and after-hours triage:
   - urgent callouts route to owner or on-call number when included
   - non-urgent after-hours enquiries get instant reply and callback booking
   - high-risk or pricing-sensitive requests do not get promised by automation

6. Qualify:
   - job type, location, urgency, photos, preferred time, service area

7. Book:
   - callback, site visit, quote request, calendar booking

8. Follow Up:
   - no-response, quote follow-up, old leads, reviews

9. Owner view:
   - what the owner sees and what they do not need to manage

10. Customer experience:
   - customer gets fast reply and clear next step

11. CTA:
   - "Book a Free Front Desk Audit"

Image guidance:

- booking calendar mockup

## Standard CTA Flow

- top-page CTA: Book Free Audit
- mid-page CTA: See How It Works or Find The Right Package
- bottom-page CTA: Book Free Audit

Avoid adding multiple competing primary CTAs. The audit is the main conversion path.

## Booking Flow

The booking flow should be direct and practical.

Steps:

1. Visitor clicks "Book Free Audit."
2. Visitor lands on audit page.
3. Visitor reads what the audit checks.
4. Visitor completes short qualification form.
5. Visitor chooses a calendar time.
6. Confirmation page explains what to prepare.
7. Reminder SMS/email goes out.
8. Audit call happens.
9. Follow-up summary and recommendation sent.

Confirmation page copy:

> Your Free Front Desk Audit is booked. Before the call, think about where enquiries currently come from, how missed calls are handled, how quotes are followed up, and whether you have old leads or past customers sitting in a list.

## Tracking Events

Track:

- audit CTA clicks

- pricing CTA clicks

- contact form submissions
- lead source

- trade selected

## Website Copy Rules

Every section should answer one of these:

- What problem does this solve?
- Why should a tradie care?
- How does this save time?
- How does this help book more work?
- Why is this different from leads, ads, or software?
- What should they do next?

If a section only explains technology, rewrite it.
